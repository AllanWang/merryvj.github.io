Pluto: a concept for a Gen-Z digital platform

Collaborators: Sophia Patterson, Michael Glushakov

Timeframe: October 2019

Tools: Figma

What if there was a place to curate all your internet content?

From our favorite memes to Medium articles, what we consume online is part of our Millenial/Gen-Z identities. But besides tagging friends in posts, or messaging them a link or screenshot, there's no good way for us to share these fragments with the people closest to us.

As part of joining Catalyst, a social and pre-professional organization for tech at Duke, my team and I conceptualized and pitched our idea for a platform we oh-so-cutely named Pluto.

Pinterest pins + Reddit subs + Tumblr energy = ?

Pluto allows users to aggregate media (such as photos, blogs, videos...and tiktoks?) from other sites. They can curate content alone for themselves or in a shared space with other friends. The goal is for users to be able to look back at what they've curated – with the same type of pride as creating the perfect Spotify playlist.


Brainstorming

My team spent 2 whiteboarding sessions defining key user flows and interactions. We tried to answer the following questions:

1. How might we organize posts? A feed? Trello-card-style? Or...?
2. How do we signal the value of a post? Points? Visual goodies?
3. What is the easiest way for a user to add a new "post"?
4. What would social interaction look like? Comments? Chatroom?


One of our challenges was the increasing complexity of our idea – we were even getting ourselves confused! As we were brainstorming we fleshed out an idea of using astrological bodies in space as a mental model. As a user and a designer, I love the extension of metaphors (Desktop folders, Pinterest Pins!).

Navigating with an astrological mental model

Instead of sticking with a classic feed (or even grid view) for displaying content, we experimented with something new. I wondered if we were trading usability for creativity, but nonetheless it was definitely a fun design exercise!
