Improve retention

The redesign of the "Search" tab is part of larger efforts to improve first week retention. We saw that after onboarding, most people go directly to that tab to find relevant content. But many run into disappointing results and never come back.

Understand

Problems with the existing design

  • Disappointing query results - The design of the filter selection encourages people to punch in multiple selectors that return little-to-no relevant results. Content on the app is limited – on purpose.
  • It's tedious - Anecdotally from reviews, people feel like the search functionality is too effortful to use.
  • Don't know what is available - Especially for first-time users, they have little knowledge of the type of content on the app. And if a filter or search query does not return anything relevant, people will modify the query and try again multiple times to no avail.

Insights from usage data

  • Try search first - The majority of new and old users both make a beeline for the search tab on first launch. Filtering happens 3x more than using the search bar. Many search bar queries are also very specific e.g. "animation cafe."
  • Location, location, location - Half of all filters and searches are for regions. Many are for destinations like Busan and Jeju Island (outside of Seoul) and the rest are smaller districts or neighbourhoods within Seoul.
  • Long-term planning AND short-term "emergencies" - People go to the search tab both to plan out a weekend trip (e.g. to Busan) and when trying to find nearby places on-the-go. This was a surprise! The Home tab was originally designed to serve the planning use cases – and the Search tab the ASAP use case.

Filtering and search activity changed significantly (increased 40% and decreased 10%, respectively).
